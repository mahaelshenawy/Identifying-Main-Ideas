Welcome to Main Ideas Mastery!

Learn to find the main idea using 5 powerful clues:

  • 1. Pictures - Visual clues about the topic and details
  • 2. Titles - Often reveal what the reading is about
  • 3. Topic Sentences - Usually the first sentence of a paragraph
  • 4. Supporting Details - Information that proves the author's point
  • 5. Repeated Words - Show what the author thinks is important

Let's become reading experts!

Clue 1: Pictures Tell Stories

Pictures: Visual images that show you what the reading is about (the topic) before you read. Pictures can also show supporting details.

Pictures help us understand the main topic at a glance

Important Tips About Pictures:

  • Position matters: A picture near the top or bottom usually shows the general topic
  • Specific details: A picture beside a paragraph usually goes with a specific detail
  • Visual clues: Look at what is happening in the picture to understand the main idea

Clue 2: Titles Guide Us

Title: The heading of a text that often tells you what the reading will be about.

Titles are like signposts that guide us

How Titles Help:

  • Non-fiction: Titles usually tell you exactly what the text is about
  • Fiction: Be careful! Titles in stories don't always help find the main idea
  • Example: A text titled "The Benefits of Exercise" tells you the topic immediately
💡 Tip: Always read the title first. It's your first clue about what you will learn!

Clue 3: Topic Sentences

Topic Sentence: The first sentence of a paragraph that usually tells you the main idea of that paragraph.

The first sentence often contains the main idea

Key Information:

  • Location: Usually found at the beginning of a paragraph
  • Scope: It is the main idea of ONE paragraph, not a whole book
  • General: Topic sentences are general statements, not specific details

Example:

"Coffee is one of the most popular drinks in the world."

This is the topic sentence. The rest of the paragraph will explain why coffee is popular.

Clue 4: Supporting Details

Supporting Details: Information that proves the author's point and explains the main idea.

Details support and explain the main idea

How to Find Supporting Details:

  • Collect the details: Write down all the specific information in the text
  • Look for patterns: Find a common theme among the details
  • They explain: Supporting details answer "Why?" "How?" or "What examples?"

Example:

Main Idea: Coffee is popular
Supporting Details:
  • It is grown in many countries
  • People drink it in the morning
  • It brings people together in cafes

Clue 5: Repeated Words

Repeated Words: Words or phrases that appear multiple times in a text. These words give clues about what the author thinks is most important.

Repeated words show what is important

How to Use Repeated Words:

  • Look throughout: Check the beginning, middle, and end of the text
  • Count them: The more a word repeats, the more important it is
  • Ask why: "Why does the author keep mentioning this word?"

Example:

"Exercise is important. Regular exercise strengthens your heart. Exercise improves mental health. Daily exercise is recommended."

The word "exercise" is repeated 4 times! This tells us that exercise is the main topic.

Using All 5 Clues Together

When you read, use these clues together to find the main idea:

Clue What to Look For
1. Picture Visual representation of the topic or details
2. Title The heading that introduces the subject
3. Topic Sentence First sentence of a paragraph (usually the main idea)
4. Supporting Details Specific information that explains the main idea
5. Repeated Words Words that appear multiple times (show importance)
🎯 Strategy: Don't rely on just one clue. Use all 5 together for the best results!

Quick Reference: The 5 Clues

1. PICTURES

Show the topic and supporting details. Position matters: top/bottom = general topic, beside text = specific detail.

2. TITLES

Tell you what the reading is about. Be careful with fiction—titles don't always help find the main idea.

3. TOPIC SENTENCES

Usually the first sentence of a paragraph. It is the main idea of ONE paragraph, not the whole text.

4. SUPPORTING DETAILS

Prove the author's point. Collect details and look for a common theme. They answer "Why?" "How?" or "What examples?"

5. REPEATED WORDS

Give clues about what the author thinks is important. Look at the beginning and end of paragraphs. The more a word repeats, the more important it is.

Your Reading Strategy

When you read, follow these steps:

  1. Step 1: Look at the picture (if there is one). What does it show?
  2. Step 2: Read the title. What is the text about?
  3. Step 3: Read the first sentence (topic sentence). What is the main idea?
  4. Step 4: Look for repeated words. What does the author emphasize?
  5. Step 5: Identify supporting details. How do they explain the main idea?
  6. Step 6: Ask yourself: "What is this mostly about?" This is your main idea!
✅ Remember: Use all 5 clues together. They work as a team to help you find the main idea!

Tips for Success

You can master this skill!

  • 🎯 Read actively: Don't just read passively. Think about what you're reading.
  • 🔍 Mark repeated words: Highlight or underline words that appear multiple times.
  • 📝 Take notes: Write down the main idea and supporting details as you read.
  • 🤔 Ask questions: Always ask yourself "What is this mostly about?"
  • 🔄 Practice regularly: The more you practice, the easier it becomes!
  • 📚 Read different texts: Practice with news, stories, and educational materials.
